Using Machine Learning to Improve Bank Telemarketing¶

By Rikesh Patel¶

Our Problem¶

  • Given historical data, determine if an individual will subscribe to a term deposit following a telemarketing call

  • Term Deposit: Cash investment held over a fixed amount of time

  • Importance: More deposits = More loans

Understanding the Data¶

  • Collected from UC Irvine ML Repository

  • Each row represents a phone call made by a Portuguese bank to a potential client

45,211 phone calls with 17 attributes

  • Categorical attributes
    • Job: type of job ex: blue-collar, technician, retired, etc.
    • Marital: marital status
    • Education: education level
    • Default: do they have credit in default?
    • Housing: do they have a housing loan?
    • Loan: do they have a personal loan?
    • Month: month in which phone call was made
    • Contact: type of contact ex: cellular, telephone, unknown
    • POutcome: outcome of previous contact ex: failure, success, nonexistent
  • Numeric attributes
    • Age: client's age
    • Balance: client's account balance
    • Day: day of the month call was made
    • Duration: duration of call in seconds
    • Campaign: number of times this client was contacted during this campaign
    • PDays: number of days that passed by after the client was last contacted from a previous campaign
    • Previous: number of times this client was contacted before this campaign
  • Target Attribute(Y): Was the phone call successfull? ex: yes, no

Data Transformation¶

Balance the data¶

Undersample the majority class ('No') so we have an even amount of cases of both classes¶

In [24]:
df_majority = df[df.y=='no']
df_minority = df[df.y=='yes']
df_majority_undersampled = resample(df_majority, n_samples=df_minority.shape[0], random_state=21)
df_balanced = pd.concat([df_majority_undersampled, df_minority])

Building the Model¶

  • Considered:

    • K-Nearest Neighbors: distance based, works best with continous variables


    • Naive-Bayes: probabilistic, works best with categorical variables


    • Decision Tree: hierarchal flow-chart, works well with categorical and numerical variables
  • Model Chosen: Decision Tree

How can a bank use these results?¶

The greatest takeaway for a Bank is the important features¶

A Bank should target clients based on:

  • their job type

  • the outcome of the last time they were contacted

  • the number of days that passed since the client was contacted during the previous campaign
